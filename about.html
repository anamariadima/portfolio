Who am I?

So nice to meet you! I'm Ana, a fast learner, organized and versatile product designer coming from a diverse background spanning teaching, dental technique and traditional art.

I’m specialized in design systems and patterns, creating documentation, ensuring consistency and a seamless user experience across various and complex applications.

Other than that? ☕️

I’m a Romanian based in Prague, Czech Republic and I'm a cute and introvert human being, with a love for long walks, museums, food, binge-watching, peonies, cats and cappuccino.

What about my skills?

I'm always focused on solving the business problems by leveraging my skills in user interface, accessibility, user experience and at the same time continuously working on my development skills to better understand the dev team constraints. I am a great team player and being a design system specialist, my nature is to be transparent, inclusive, communicative, creative and always open to feedback. My work process usually goes from asking the why, research, understanding customer requirements, A/B testing, rapid sketching up to ready for development designs and providing support and detailed documentation.

What are my values?

First of all, always be kind. We're all human, and building strong connections with others can positively impact our work and contribute to the overall health of the company. Building strong relationships through trust, respect, and kindness is a significant milestone, and creating a safe space for learning, growth, and continuous improvement is an art. All above plus being humble, unlocking your potential, being organized, seeking feedback, showing empathy, and valuing people’s time are just a few of the core values I live by.

What makes me a super designer?

As a highly responsible, organized and reliable person, all I do, I do with an open mind, analysing all the feedback, user's perspective and the best practices that already make the lives of the customers easier. I'm always keeping in mind that a design is a custom solution which is very dependent on the context and the user's problems I'm trying to solve.
